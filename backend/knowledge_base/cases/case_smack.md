## О компании клиента (web research)
SmackSocial was founded in 2021 as a capstone project at Brigham Young University (BYU). The co‑founders are **Whitney Graham** (CEO), **Kent Broadbent**, and initially **James Stevens** as a third partner during inception ([utahbusiness.com]).

 SmackSocial is headquartered in **Provo, Utah**, and currently operates with a small team—estimated at **2–10 employees** ([linkedin.com]).

 Brand‑styled as the **“Anti‑Fomo App”**, SmackSocial aims to counter the fears of missing out by creating **authentic in‑person connections** rather than passive digital consumption. It helps users easily plan real-life hangouts via temporary group chats that expire once an event concludes ([utahbusiness.com]).

 While specific funding rounds and amounts remain undisclosed, the company has **closed six‑figure investment deals**, and team members have secured **pitch and business competition wins** ([utahbusiness.com]).

SmackSocial offers a streamlined **social coordination app**—users create event‑based group chats (e.g., for movie nights or social meetups), which vanish once the hangout ends ([utahbusiness.com]).

 Designed for social hobbyists and young adults—especially Gen Z—it targets people who find coordination more stressful than the event itself. Additionally, because of BYU’s origin, early users likely include university students and community groups ([utahbusiness.com]).
