## О компании клиента (web research)
bitFlyer was founded on January 9, 2014, by Yuzo Kano, a former Goldman Sachs derivatives and bonds trader. The company launched its cryptocurrency exchange in April 2014 in Japan. ([en.wikipedia.org]) 

 bitFlyer’s mission is “making the world simpler with blockchain,” empowering millions globally to access cryptocurrencies; this phrasing appears in a May 8, 2025 blog related to its U.S. operations. ([blog.bitflyer.com]) 

 - By 2018, bitFlyer had around 150 employees. ([en.wikipedia.org]) 
 - Employee count and current revenue figures beyond that are not found in public sources. 

 - As of data collected, bitFlyer has raised approximately **$35.71 million** over **10 financing rounds**. ([cbinsights.com]) 
 - Notable rounds include: 
 - **Series C** in April 2016: ¥3 billion (~$27 million), led by SBI Investment, including Venture Labo, raising total to ~$34.9 million at that point. ([globalventuring.com]) 
 - **Series D** on February 14, 2017: raised $1.76 million, with investors such as Dai-ichi Life, Mizuho Financial Group, and Sumitomo. ([cbinsights.com]) 
 - More recent financings include “Acquired” on May 31, 2023 by D&I Investment, and “Acq - Fin” April 4, 2022 by ACA Investments, but amounts/valuations are unspecified. ([cbinsights.com]) 
 - Earlier funding included JPY 130 million (~$1.2 million) in early rounds from RSP Fund No. 5, GMO Venture Partners, and Bitcoin Opportunity Corp for expansion and hiring. ([bitflyer.com]) 

 - Founder & CEO: Yuzo Kano. ([en.wikipedia.org]) 
 - Key Executive: Kuniyoshi Hayashi, CEO (possibly of a specific regional entity or overall leadership). ([en.wikipedia.org])

- Traditional crypto exchange includes spot trading, futures contracts, and derivatives – as of 2017, derivatives made up ~75% of transactions. ([en.wikipedia.org]) 
 - Blockchain platform “Miyabi” launched in 2016, supporting smart contracts. ([en.wikipedia.org]) 
 - Expanded asset support to include Ethereum and Litecoin by 2019. ([en.wikipedia.org]) 
 - Introduced crypto payments at retail, purchases via credit cards and loyalty points. ([en.wikipedia.org]) 
 - Launched “bvote,” a blockchain voting service, and a consulting arm in 2020. ([en.wikipedia.org]) 

 - May 15, 2025: Temporarily suspended Bitcoin Cash (BCH) deposits/withdrawals due to network upgrade; trading unaffected. ([blog.bitflyer.com]) 
 - Post-acquisition of FTX Japan (2024): rebranded as “Custody New Company” by August 2024, focusing on institutional-grade crypto custody and preparing for future crypto ETFs. ([cryptonews.net]) 

 - Broad: individual Japanese users (including derivatives traders), institutional investors (post-FTX Japan acquisition), and retail businesses seeking crypto payment options. ([en.wikipedia.org])
