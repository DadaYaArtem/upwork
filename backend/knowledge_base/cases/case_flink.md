## О компании клиента (web research)
- Founded in **late 2020** in Berlin by **Oliver Merkel**, **Christoph Cordes**, and **Julian Dames**; later joined by **Saad Saeed** and **Nikolas Bullwinkel** through the acquisition of Pickery ([en.wikipedia.org]).
- Mission/Vision: While not explicitly stated as a formal mission, Flink positions itself as Europe’s leading **quick-commerce** service—delivering groceries within minutes via hyper-local dark stores using company-employed e-bike couriers ([en.wikipedia.org]).
- Company size: Approximately **12,000 employees** as of 2021; increased to around **13,000 employees by May 2023** ([en.wikipedia.org]).
- Revenue: Reported revenues of around **€400 million in 2022** ([de.wikipedia.org]).
- Funding history:
 - Seed round: ~**€10 million**, valuation ~€30 million ([ideausher.com]).
 - March 2021: **$52 million** funding, expansion into Netherlands & France ([techcrunch.com]).
 - Mid‑2021: **$240 million** funding; valuation surpassed **$1 billion** (unicorn) ([thebrandhopper.com]).
 - December 2021: ~**$750 million** round, valuation ~**$2.1–3 billion** ([en.wikipedia.org]).
 - May 2023: **€150 million** raise (equity €115M, debt €35M), valuation down to ~**€1 billion** ([de.wikipedia.org]).
 - March 2026: **$100 million** growth capital from Prosus, Btomorrow Ventures, and others ([tech.eu]).
- Key leadership: Founders with roles: Oliver Merkel (CEO), Christoph Cordes, Julian Dames; Saad Saeed and Nikolas Bullwinkel are Directors of Technology and Experience respectively ([ideausher.com]).

- Main products/services:
 - Quick-commerce grocery delivery via **dark stores** in hyper-local hubs across Germany and the Netherlands; previously also in France (now liquidated) ([en.wikipedia.org]).
 - Offers a wide range: fresh produce, meat, bakery, dairy, snacks, alcohol, household essentials, and meal kits/prepared meals ([thebrandhopper.com]).
- Recent product launches/updates (2025–2026):
 - March 2026: Raised funds to fuel **targeted expansion** and open additional fulfilment hubs focused on profitability and density ([tech.eu]).
- Target customers/user base:
 - Reached **15 million households** in Germany (plus total reach of **22.5 million** households in Germany and the Netherlands) ([na.linkedin.com]).
- Pricing model:
 - Delivery fee as low as **€1.80** in Germany; slight markup on grocery prices overall ([ideausher.com]).
- Key integrations/partnerships:
 - **REWE Group** as exclusive supplier in Germany ([thebrandhopper.com]).
 - Acquisition of **Cajoo** (France) in May 2022 (~€100 million); Carrefour became a shareholder and exclusive partner in France ([en.wikipedia.org]).
 - **Wolt** partnership (Jan 2023): Wolt couriers delivering ~2,300 Flink products in overlapping markets ([startbase.com]).
 - **UberEats** integration since February 2024 in Germany ([de.wikipedia.org]).
 - Financing partnership via **REWE Group** in Sept 2024 alongside a partnership with Just Eat Takeaway ([ypog.law]).

- Tech stack: Not explicitly documented in public sources. No reliable info from job listings, BuiltWith, or GitHub.
- Engineering team size: No data found.

- Funding: March 3, 2026: **$100 million** for expansion and profitability ([tech.eu]).
- Partnership: September 2024: Financing with REWE plus preferred partnership with Just Eat Takeaway ([ypog.law]).
- Acquisitions:
 - May 2022: Acquisition of **Cajoo** (~€100 million), with Carrefour involvement; French ops later liquidated by April 2024 ([en.wikipedia.org]).
- Product milestones: Reaching **22.5 million households** in Germany & Netherlands by adding 2.3 million new households ([na.linkedin.com]).
- Expansion: Focusing on **selective hub openings** in profitable, dense German regions in 2026 ([tech.eu]).
- Hiring trends: LinkedIn indicates scale — company size of 5,001–10,000 employees, open roles ongoing ([na.linkedin.com]).
