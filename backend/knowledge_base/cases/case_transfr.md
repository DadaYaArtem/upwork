## О компании клиента (web research)
Transfr, Inc. (also known as Transfr or TransfrVR) was founded in **2017** by **Bharanidharan Rajakumar** in **New York City** ([edsurge.com]).

 Transfr’s mission is to train individuals for **well-paying, high-demand careers**, particularly in fields that do not require four-year degrees, using immersive VR training to foster workforce development and upward mobility ([businesswire.com]).

 Employee estimates vary:
 • Gaebler lists Transfr’s team size as between **101–250** employees ([crunchbase.com]). 
 • Bounce Watch indicates around **286** employees ([bouncewatch.com]). 
 No reliable public revenue data was found. 

 Transfr has raised funding across multiple rounds totaling between **$85M and $95M**: 
 • **Seed**: ~$5.5M in May 2019 ([jobo.world]). 
 • **Series A**: $12M in November 2020 ([edsurge.com]). 
 • **Series B**: ~$35M in June 2022 ([jobo.world]). 
 • **Series C**: $40M in September 2023, led by ABS Capital Partners, together with JPMorgan Chase, Lumos Capital Group, Akkadian Ventures, Album VC, Spring Tide Capital, and others ([jobo.world]). 
 Total funding estimates range from **$85.7M** (Jobo) to **$95.37M** (Forge) ([jobo.world]).

 • **Founder & CEO**: Bharanidharan Rajakumar ([edsurge.com]). 
 • **CTO**: Fabien Chazot ([gaebler.com]).

• **Transfr Trek**: Launched March 2025, an XR-based, multi-modal career exploration solution integrating mobile, VR, and curriculum resources to support middle/high school, community college, nonprofit, and workforce development programs. Offers support in English, Spanish, and closed captioning, with over **330 interactive training simulations** across eight high-growth sectors, reaching **400,000+ learners** ([businesswire.com]). 
 • **Career Exploration Web App**: Offers mobile-enabled career profiles—over 100 careers—with immersive VR “day-in-the-life” experiences ([mdi.org]).

 • **Transfr Trek** debuted in early 2025 ([businesswire.com]). 
 • **MDI Partnership** (March 27, 2025): Launched VR career exploration for people with disabilities under the “Unified Work” philanthropic initiative, featuring 30+ work experiences in sectors like manufacturing, health sciences, architecture, logistics, agriculture ([mdi.org]). 
 • **Etex LINK Program** (November 2025): Launched the “Learning Immersively for Next‑Gen Knowledge” initiative in rural Texas to train high school students and incumbent workers in broadband and telecommunications via immersive VR, awarding FOA-aligned microcredentials in fiber optics roles ([etex.net]).
