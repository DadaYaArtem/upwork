## Проблема клиента
Penn Vet is a brand-new veterinarian application that we developed from scratch to help the client realize his idea for further usage. The app is designed for animal shelters that constantly struggle with documentation. It contains all the needed features to make the work of shelters easy and convenient.

## Решение
Veterinarian application for animal shelters that struggle with documentation. Penn Vet is for the Shelter Medicine Program at University of Pennsylvania School of Veterinary Medicine

Задачи команды: Backend - PHP & Laravel. Frontend - React Native

## О компании клиента (web research)
- **Founded**: Established in 1884 following a $10,000 donation from trustee Joshua Ballinger Lippincott in 1882 and formal opening on October 2, 1884 ([vet.upenn.edu]).
- **Mission / Vision**: Mission is to train veterinary leaders advancing healthcare outcomes, global health, sustainable agriculture, interdisciplinary careers, and wellbeing in the profession. Vision is “A world with exceptional and vastly more integrated health of animals, people, and the environment.” Values include leadership, collaboration, interdisciplinarity, opportunity, sustainability ([vet.upenn.edu]).
 - Treats over 59,000 animals annually ([vet.upenn.edu]).
 - Ryan Hospital handles over 31,000 cases yearly; large-animal hospital visiting ~6,700; on-farm services ~22,000 animals across 183,854 miles ([pennvetproud.org]).
 - Performs ~143,000 diagnostic tests annually and tracks outbreaks among 114 million birds and 1.5 million pigs ([pennvetproud.org]).
- **Funding History**: Public funding includes a long-standing state appropriation dating back to 1889 (e.g., $25,000 then). Recent attempts to secure ~$31M–$33M in state funding were blocked in December 2023 due to legislative fallout, with unresolved status as of mid‑2024 ([vet.upenn.edu]).
 - Current dean: Andrew M. Hoffman, DVM, DVSc, DACVIM (term 2018–present) ([vet.upenn.edu]).
 - Board of Advisors chaired by Mark D. Spitzer; includes vice‑chair Martin E. Winter and various professionals ([vet.upenn.edu]).
 - Dean’s Council leadership includes Associate Deans (e.g., Katrin Hinrichs, Olena Jacenko, Jennifer Punt) and Vice Deans (e.g., Phillip Scott, Robert Schieri) ([vet.upenn.edu]).

---

- Two campuses: Philadelphia (Matthew J. Ryan Veterinary Hospital—companion animals) and Kennett Square (New Bolton Center—large animals) ([en.wikipedia.org]).
 - Established PennHIP program for diagnosing canine hip dysplasia, founded by Gail Smith in 1993; transitioned to nonprofit, now commercialized as AIS PennHIP ([en.wikipedia.org]).
 - 2022: First successful equine IVF resulting in viable foals ([vet.upenn.edu]).
 - 2024: Successful AAV gene therapy in dogs with hemophilia demonstrating lasting efficacy ([vet.upenn.edu]).
 - Shelter Medicine Program (since 2006) with mobile outreach initiative funded (~$1.5M in 2017) ([almanac.upenn.edu]).
 - Part of One Health interdisciplinary efforts, including centers: Wildlife Futures, Working Dog Center, Institute for Infectious and Zoonotic Diseases, Cancer Center, Center for Germline Cells, etc. ([vet.upenn.edu]).
- **Target Users**: Veterinary students, academic researchers, animal welfare organizations, state agriculture bodies, pet owners, and large‑animal practitioners.
 - Collaborates with PA Dept. of Agriculture, PA Game Commission, PA Animal Diagnostic Laboratory System, national lab networks ([onehealth.upenn.edu]).
 - Mobile clinic funded by Bernice Barbour Foundation, PetSmart Charities, and Wiederhold Foundation ([almanac.upenn.edu]).

---

- **State Funding Unrest**: December 2023 state funding (~$31M–$33M) blocked; by April 2024 still unresolved; appeals for 2023–24 and 2024–25 funding continuing ([cbsnews.com]).
- **Clinic Closure**: A Penn veterinary clinic serving veterans abruptly closed, with funding denied amid political backlash; alternative funding attempts in mid‑2024 failed ([thedp.com]).
- **Strategic Framework 2025–2030**: New roadmap “United by Purpose. Built for Impact.” outlines modernization of teaching hospitals, launching community health clinic, new centers of excellence, expanding specialty training, community wellness programs, and social work initiatives ([vet.upenn.edu]).
