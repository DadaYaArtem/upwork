## О компании клиента (web research)
Erste Group traces its roots to the founding of *Erste österreichische Spar‑Casse* on **4 October 1819** in Vienna by the priest **Johann Baptist Weber**; the charter explicitly committed to inclusion regardless of age, gender, social standing, or nationality ([erstegroup.com]). The current holding company, **Erste Group Bank AG**, was established on **9 August 2008**, splitting from Erste Bank Oesterreich and consolidating foreign subsidiaries under a single entity ([en.wikipedia.org]).

 The original founding purpose was to democratize access to savings and prosperity for all ([erstegroup.com]). More recently, Erste Group states it’s transitioning from being solely a financial services provider to **"building Financial Health"** in its regions, emphasizing social, ecological, and ethical principles under binding guidelines like its Code of Conduct ([erstegroup.com]).

 - Employees: approximately **45,700–45,900** as of late 2024/early 2025 ([en.wikipedia.org]). 
 - Clients: around **16 million** across seven Central and Eastern European countries ([en.wikipedia.org]). 
 - Branches: over **2,000** locations ([en.wikipedia.org]). 
 - 2023 operating income: **€10.552 billion**; net income: **€7.228 billion**; equity: **€28.5 billion**; assets: **€362.851 billion** as of Q2 2025 ([en.wikipedia.org]). 
 - Forbes reports for 2025 list revenue at approximately **$25.1 billion**, assets around **$366.9 billion**, and profits near **$3.2 billion** ([forbes.com]).

 Erste Group went **public in 1997**, listing on the Vienna, Prague, and Bucharest stock exchanges ([en.wikipedia.org]). No significant external funding rounds were found beyond its IPO.

 - Current CEO: **Peter Bosek**, appointed from **July 2024** ([de.wikipedia.org]). 
 - Other board members include **Stefan Dörfler**, **Alexandra Habeler‑Drabek**, **Ingo Bleier**, and **Maurizio Poletto** ([en.wikipedia.org]).

Erste Group offers a broad suite including retail and commercial banking, investment and private banking, and asset management ([en.wikipedia.org]).

 - **George**: a digital banking platform launched in Austria in 2015, later expanded to other CEE markets. By end of 2023, it had **~10 million users** ([en.wikipedia.org]). 
 - **George Business**: introduced in 2023; a corporate banking platform offering financial health assessments and data-driven ratings for businesses ([de.wikipedia.org]). 
 - **George Invest**: launched in 2025 in Austria and Czech Republic, targeting younger investors. By year-end 2025, nearly **two million securities savings plans** were managed – a 25% increase over 2024; youth (under 35) represent 62% of securities transactions in Austria and 33% in Czech Republic ([erstegroup.com]).

 Platforms like George and George Invest clearly target retail and younger customer segments; George Business serves corporates. Overall client base spans retail, corporate, and public sectors across CEE.
