## Проблема клиента
15 Seconds of Fame (15SOF) is a simple way for fans to receive their video appearances from live events. The 15SOF app will deliver users any appearance from TV or the Jumbotron, where 15SOF has partnerships.

## Решение

## О компании клиента (web research)
15SOF was founded in 2014 by Adam B. Resnick and is headquartered in Santa Monica, California ([en.wikipedia.org]).

 The company’s vision centers around enhancing live-event experiences by using AI and facial recognition to identify fans captured on video boards or broadcasts—and deliver those moments to their phones as keepsakes. They aim to transform fleeting live moments into "social gold," scaled for user shareability, rights holder fan engagement, and brand sponsorships ([sportsbusinessjournal.com]).

 Employee estimates vary: BounceWatch reports approximately 38 employees ([bouncewatch.com]), while a Sports Tech report estimates 51–100 employees ([xtventures.com]).

 In May 2024, 15SOF secured a $15 million Series A funding round led by Shadowbox Studios, with Bridger Holdings also participating. MLB and the NBA hold minority equity stakes in the company, obtained in the prior two years ([sportsbusinessjournal.com]).

 - **Founder/CSO**: Adam B. Resnick ([en.wikipedia.org]) 
 - **Advisory Board**: Kobe Bryant (joined 2017) ([en.wikipedia.org]) 
 - **Board of Directors**: Armen Keteyian (Emmy-winning broadcaster), Bruce Cohen (former Senate Judiciary Committee lead attorney), Sandy Montag (advisor & sports media leader) ([en.wikipedia.org]).

- SDK for integration into partner apps, including MLB’s Ballpark app ([sportsbusinessjournal.com]). 
 - AFA (“Audience From Anywhere”): virtual-audience platform created for The Drew Barrymore Show during the pandemic ([sportsbusinessjournal.com]). 
 - Patented “tagless video” sharing—automatically shares captured clips to friends featured in videos, pending user privacy preferences ([sportsbusinessjournal.com]). 

 No specific new launches identified post‑2025. Public sources do not mention updates in 2025–2026 ([15sof.com]).

 Targets include rights holders, teams, venues, broadcasters and brands—especially leagues like MLB, NBA, NHL, NFL, MLS, NCAA, plus concert and entertainment event organizers ([sportsbusinessjournal.com]). User demographics: 57% fans in their 20s and 30s ([sportsbusinessjournal.com]).

 The app is free for fans and teams; revenue is generated primarily through sponsorships tied to delivered content ([sportsbusinessjournal.com]).

 - SDK embedded in MLB Ballpark app ([sportsbusinessjournal.com]). 
 - Partnerships with major leagues: NBA (minority stake, 2022), MLB (renewed + equity stake, 2023) ([en.wikipedia.org]). 
 - Collaborations with Big Ten Network (collegiate spaces) ([sportsbusinessjournal.com]). 
 - Teams: New Jersey Devils, Brooklyn Nets via YinzCam, Atlanta Hawks (integration in team app) ([atlantahawkspr.wordpress.com]). 
 - Clients: Las Vegas Golden Knights, Cleveland Cavaliers, Nashville Predators ([xtventures.com]).
